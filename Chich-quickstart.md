# Our Recipes, Comrade — Your Quick Start

This is everything you need. You only ever do one thing: **edit recipes in Obsidian.**
Everything else (saving to GitHub, updating our website) happens automatically in the
background. You never need to touch a terminal, type a command, or think about "git."

---

## The one rule

Edit recipes **only in Obsidian**. That's it. When you make a change, it saves and
syncs on its own within a few minutes. When you open Obsidian, it automatically pulls
in any changes I've made too.

You don't need to "save to GitHub" or press any sync button — it's set to do that by
itself. (If you ever see a little number badge or a "sync" indicator in the corner,
that's normal; it's just the automatic backup doing its job.)

## Opening the recipes

1. Open the **Obsidian** app.
2. Our recipe collection opens as a "vault." If it's not already open, choose it from
   the vault switcher (the icon in the bottom-left).
3. On the left you'll see folders like `breakfast`, `dinner`, `baking`. Click any
   recipe to open it.

## Reading a recipe

Each recipe shows a clean view with the ingredients, steps, and timers. There may be a
**preview/edit toggle** (top-right corner) — preview shows the nice formatted version,
edit shows the raw text you can change.

## Editing or adding a recipe

To **change** a recipe, just click into it and type. Done.

To **add** a new one:

1. Right-click a folder → **New file** (or use the new-note button).
2. Give it a name ending in **`.cook`** — for example, `banana-bread.cook`.
3. Type the recipe using the simple format below.

That's all. It'll sync automatically.

---

## The recipe format (it's just three symbols)

Start with a little info block between two `---` lines, then write the recipe in plain
English with three small markers.

```
---
title: Banana Bread
servings: 8
---
Mash @bananas{3} in a #bowl. Mix in @sugar{1%cup} and @melted butter{100%g}.
Fold in @flour{2%cups} and a pinch of @salt.
Pour into a #loaf pan{} and bake for ~{55%minutes}.
```

What the symbols mean:

- **`@`** before an **ingredient**. If it's more than one word, put `{}` right after it:
  `@brown sugar{}`. Put the amount inside the braces: `@flour{2%cups}` means 2 cups of
  flour. The `%` separates the number from the unit.
- **`#`** before **equipment** (a pot, pan, bowl). More than one word? Add `{}`:
  `#loaf pan{}`.
- **`~`** before a **timer**: `~{55%minutes}`.

Don't have an amount for something? Just write `@salt` with nothing after it.

If something looks wrong in the preview, you probably just missed a `{}` after a
two-word name — add it and it'll fix itself.
